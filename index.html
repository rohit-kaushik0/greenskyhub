Innovative Green Aviation Technologies

Hydrogen-Powered Aircraft

Hydrogen planes emit only water vapor and are ideal for long flights. Challenges include safe hydrogen storage, large storage tanks due to low energy density, and fossil fuel dependency in production.

All Details

Electric Aircraft

Electric planes reduce emissions and noise, perfect for short regional flights. Limited battery capacity restricts them to shorter routes, posing challenges in production and disposal.

All Details

Biofuel-Powered Aircraft

Biofuel planes, using organic sources, cut greenhouse gases for both short and long flights. Scaling up biofuel production and sustainable sourcing are key challenges.

INDIA'S PATH TO GREEN AVIATION

India's aviation industry is rapidly embracing green practices, targeting net zero carbon emissions by 2050.
The focus is on sustainable aviation fuels (SAF) and fuel-efficient planes, with a goal of 10% SAF blend by 2030.
Key partnerships, such as Indian Oil Corporation and LanzaJet, highlight significant progress in this direction.
These initiatives not only address environmental concerns but also generate employment, establishing India as a leader in global green aviation efforts.
